Crate & Sleep Guide
Sleeping Through the Night
Week by week — what to expect
▼
Every night — the routine always do this
🚽
Last toilet trip as late as possibleTake him out at 10–11pm. Wait until he goes, then praise warmly.
🏠
Crate near your bed for weeks 1–4He needs to hear and smell you. Isolation at night makes crying far worse.
🧣
Worn clothing inside the crateA T-shirt with your scent. Replace every few days.
🔇
Cover the crateBlanket over the top and 3 sides — dark and den-like helps him settle faster.
Week 8 — first nights what to expect
😢
He will cry. This is completely normal.He has left his mum and siblings. The crying is communication, not naughtiness.
⏰
Expect 1–2 wake-ups per nightHis bladder holds roughly 3–4 hours at 8 weeks. A 2am and 5am wake-up is normal.
🤫
Night toilet trips — keep them boringLights low. No talking. No eye contact. No cuddles. Straight outside, wait, back in crate.
📉
It improves every nightNight 1 is hardest. Most puppies are down to one wake-up by week 2.
Weeks 9–11 the goal
✅
Push last toilet trip laterAn 11pm trip may mean he sleeps to 5–6am.
🌟
Sleeping through by 12–14 weeks is realisticMost puppies manage 10pm–6am with consistent crate training.
❌ Never break these rules

Never open the crate while he is actively crying — wait for 2 seconds of quiet first.

Never take him into your bed — very hard to undo.

Never skip the late-night toilet trip — accidents in the crate set back training significantly.
